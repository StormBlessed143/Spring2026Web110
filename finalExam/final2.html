Festival Fires Shine Beneath the White Tree

At sunrise beneath the gleaming White Tower, trumpets rang across Minas Tirith as King Elessar unveiled a bold decree restoring the ancient highways of Arnor and Gondor. Cheers thundered through the city while merchants flooded the lower circles with rare spices, jeweled fabrics, and treasures carried from distant realms. Rangers returning from Ithilien brought troubling whispers of shadowed creatures prowling the eastern woods, stirring unease among the people. Yet hope burns brightly still — tonight, thousands will gather beneath the blossoming White Tree for a grand festival of song, firelight, and remembrance, as minstrels recount the heroic deeds that forged the Reunited Kingdom...

As dusk fell, lanterns were lit from the Citadel down to the lowest gate, until the White City shimmered like a river of stars beneath the mountain. The White Tree itself stood at the heart of the celebration, its pale blossoms glowing in the torchlight as if answering the songs rising around it. From the courts of the upper circles to the crowded streets below, voices mingled in old lays of Númenor, of the Fellowship, and of the long struggle against the Shadow.

King Elessar was seen among the people without herald or guard, walking quietly through the gathered crowds while listening to the songs of Gondor’s rebirth. Queen Arwen, veiled in silver light, was said to have blessed the first bloom upon the White Tree before withdrawing to the upper courts. Witnesses describe a hush falling each time the White Tree’s petals were stirred by the wind, as though the city itself held its breath in reverence.

Yet not all omens were easily dismissed. Riders from the East Road reported flickering movements beyond the Rammas Echor, and some among the guards spoke in lowered voices of “old things” stirring where the land has not yet fully healed. Such reports, however, were quickly drowned by song and celebration as the night deepened.

When midnight came, a great fire was kindled in the third circle, and its light climbed the stone like living gold. There, beneath the White Tree’s watching branches, the people of Gondor pledged remembrance for the fallen and resolve for the age ahead. And as the final chorus rose into the night air, it was said that even the wind fell still, as though Middle-earth itself listened and remembered.

Shirefolk Cause Stir in Lower Circles of Minas Tirith

Unseasoned visitors from the Shire caused brief commotion yesterday in the lower circles of Minas Tirith after what city guards described as “an enthusiastic misunderstanding involving produce and property lines.” The four hobbits, newly arrived as part of a goodwill procession, were reportedly seen distributing foodstuffs without proper market licensing and attempting to “improve the atmosphere” of a public stairway with painted floral designs.

Citizens initially welcomed the visitors with curiosity, but confusion grew when a local stallkeeper discovered his inventory had been “sampled for quality assurance” across several competing baskets. Guards intervened before the situation escalated further, though witnesses insist the hobbits were “entirely well-meaning, if somewhat fast-moving.”

The King’s Office has confirmed no charges will be pressed, citing cultural misunderstanding and the recent celebration of the White Tree Festival. The hobbits were last seen offering apologies, assisting in the return of missing goods, and quietly praising the city’s “excellent second breakfasts.”

Officials advise visiting Shirefolk to remain accompanied by local guides when navigating the steeper districts of the White City.

White Rider Spotted on the Great Road Toward Minas Tirith

Eyewitnesses along the Pelennor have reported the swift approach of a lone figure upon a great white horse, moving faster than any ordinary steed of Rohan or Gondor. The rider, clad in weathered grey and bearing a staff, was swiftly identified by sentries as Mithrandir, long absent from the councils of the White City.

He was seen riding Shadowfax, Lord of Horses, whose pace is said to rival the wind itself. Together they crossed the plains in a blur of white against green and gold, drawing the attention of guards stationed upon the outer walls. One watcher described the sight as “a streak of light where none should have been,” before horns were sounded to announce his approach.

No escort accompanied the pair, nor did they pause for parley at the outer garrisons. Instead, they pressed directly toward the gates of Minas Tirith with evident urgency, as though bearing tidings too swift for delay. The White City responded in kind, and the Great Gate was opened without hesitation.

What counsel or warning the White Rider brings remains unspoken at this hour, yet among the people there is a familiar stirring of anticipation. For in Gondor, it is said that Mithrandir does not return lightly—and where he rides, change is never far behind.
